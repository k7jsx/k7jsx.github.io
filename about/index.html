About

2025-06-13

Synopsys⌗

This is the radio blog for operator K7JSX.

I’m intending to keep it up to date with new or noteworthy events and milestones with the secondary goal of cataloging information as I go.

About Me⌗

I started my technology journey early on with hobby electonic component kits, modding consoles and building PCs in the late 90’s and early 00’s. I’ve been connected to the internet since the 56k era and still continue to enjoy computer networking.

Yes, I’ve installed Gentoo on an OG Xbox. It was awesome.

It was through the lense of tinkering and the desire to learn that I discovered amateur radio…

Ham Journey⌗

Seeing SOTA operators such as wGØAT and others on Youtube bring voices (or cw) out of the atmosphere was facinating. How was this possible? Could I learn to do this? Of course!

This gave me a goal: Learn how to do this well.

Technician⌗

I earned my Technician license in 2012 with the callsign KF7ZKH which didn’t last long as I would request and recieve the 1x3 vanity call of K7JSX.

Limited to VHF/UHF and no mental bandwidth available to learn CW there was not much accomplished for about a decade after this but I did manage to recruit a few close friends to earn their Technician licenses.

Learn APRS? Check.
Learn DMR? Check.
Amass an army of HTs? Check
Hear repeater drama and realize I didn’t want to engage? Check!

As the time to renew my license neared in 2023 so did my desire to get on HF. I had the time and attention span to make it happen but lacked the motivation. Each year prior I was reminded of my slipping goals by the presense of ARRL Field Day reminder on my calendar. This year was different, but I didn’t know it yet.

A fellow ham I’d met during Meshtastic experiments, KE7BUA, informed me the tests were easier to take now. The question pool was similar but there were no more #2 pencils and filling in bubbles, everything could happen on a laptop now. Approval was instant. There was no paperwork.

Ok, this is perfect. Let’s get an end fed and see if I can get on 10 meters. Let me see what the hype is about. Shortly after deploying my first antenna I completed a QSO with 3G0YA located on Easter Island. From my backyard with 5 watts, some wire and a fishing pole!

That’s it! I was hooked.

General⌗

Slammed flashcards on hamstudy.org for a few weeks and obtained my General class license on 2024.06.01, just in time for Field Day 2024. I had achieved part of my goal. I learned how to do this. Now came the harder part. Do it well. Three weeks was all I had to put together everything needed to participate.

Longer end-fed for 20m? I made my own.
Create a kit for portable ops? Done.
Sunscreen? Oops.

It was time to, you know, go out in the field. With long time friend and Technician license recuit N7DEV in tow I operated Field Day 2024 as 1B/QRP NV and logged 36 QSOs.

I’m calling it a win.

It’s been a great start to my General operating and I will one day make the final upgrade to Extra.
